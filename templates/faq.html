FAQ

How do I play the game?
The game starts in the game's lobby. Note that this is not #ircpuzzles. When a game starts, the lobby will be announced in #ircpuzzles. The namespace for the competition stays the same throughout the competition. If the namespace is "#######", all competition channels including the lobby will begin with "#######". To get to the next level, you must solve the level you are on. The next level's name is the solution to the current level. Of course, the solutions will follow IRC channel format (non case sensitive, no spaces), and begin with the competition prefix.
When does the competition start? When does it end?
The competition starts on June 28th and runs for most likely at least 48 hours. It depends on how long it takes people to finish.
What are the prizes?
The prizes can be found at http://ircpuzzles.org/prizes/. You also get a lot of practice with your cryptography and critical thinking skills!
What if I want to compete in a group?
Groups are allowed, but you will have to declare yourself as a group before the start of the competition. A form will be posted to do so. Groups may have two to three members, and may share answers among themselves. Contestants in a group are not eligible for individual awards. A group's level is the furthest any member has gotten, so if anybody in a group wins, the whole group wins.
What type of answer sharing is allowed?
Unless you are in a group and sharing with other group members, collaboration between contestants should be minimal. All competition-related channels will be moderated (+mz). The sharing of hints that have already been given before by IRC Puzzles staff is allowed, but you may not create your own hints and share those. Please don't spoil the experience for the contestants. If you do so, you will be disqualified.
